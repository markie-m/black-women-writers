Celebrating Black Women Writers

There is always light. If only we're brave enough to see it. If only we're brave enough to be it.
― Amanda Gorman

When bell hooks says, No black woman writer in this culture can write 'too much.' Indeed... no woman has ever written enough, she reminds us that, as American readers fed a diet of largely "white supremacist capitalist patriarchal" rhetoric, it's up to us to make a point to seek out, read, and otherwise support the work of Black women writers.

Let us do as the great Oprah Winfrey says and have the courage to step out of our history and past so that we can live our dreams.

I have the nerve to walk my own way, however hard, in my search for reality, rather than climb upon the rattling wagon of wishful illusions.

― Zora Neale Hurston

Courage is the most important of all the virtues because without courage, you can't practice any other virtue consistently.

― Maya Angelou

When I dare to be powerful, to use my strength in the service of my vision, then it becomes less and less important whether I am afraid.

― Audre Lorde
